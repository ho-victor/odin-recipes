Pizza Margherita

Pizza is like a big, cheesy hug in food form. It's the food that unites us all, whether you're a pepperoni lover, a veggie fanatic, or a pineapple-on-pizza rebel. It's the food that brings people together, especially when there's a big, hot, gooey pie involved. It's basically a delicious round disc of happiness that you can customize to your heart's content. So go ahead, grab a slice and feel the love (and the cheese) that pizza has to offer!

Ingredients

  1. Pizza dough: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon salt, 2/3 cup warm water, 1 teaspoon active dry yeast, 1 tablespoon olive oil: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon salt, 2/3 cup warm water, 1 teaspoon active dry yeast, 1 tablespoon olive oil
  2. San Marzano tomatoes: 1 can (28 ounces) San Marzano tomatoes, crushed or pureed
  3. Fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced or shredded: 2 cups fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced or shredded
  4. Fresh basil leaves: 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves, torn or chopped
  5. Extra-virgin olive oil: 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  6. Salt: 1 teaspoon salt

To make the pizza dough, mix together the all-purpose flour and salt in a large bowl. In a separate bowl, mix together the warm water and active dry yeast until the yeast is dissolved. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil to the flour mixture, and stir until a dough forms. Knead the dough on a floured surface until it is smooth and elastic, then place it in a greased bowl and let it rise for 1 hour.

To make the pizza, preheat your oven to 475Â°F. Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness and shape. Spread the crushed or pureed San Marzano tomatoes over the pizza dough, leaving a small border around the edge. Arrange the sliced or shredded fresh mozzarella cheese over the tomato sauce. Sprinkle the torn or chopped fresh basil leaves over the cheese. Drizzle the extra-virgin olive oil over the pizza, and sprinkle with salt. Bake the pizza for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
